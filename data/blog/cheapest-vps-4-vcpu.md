# Cheapest VPS With 4 vCPUs in 2026

**TL;DR:** Hetzner Cloud offers the lowest-cost 4-vCPU VPS at $6.29/month (CPX21), followed by Vultr at $12/month (4GB Compute). DigitalOcean's Standard 4GB ($24/month) and Akamai Linode's Linode 8GB ($48/month) cost significantly more. For managed WordPress hosting with 4 vCPUs, Cloudways starts at $47/month.

## Introduction

Finding affordable 4-vCPU virtual private servers requires comparing raw specifications across providers that have different pricing models, geographic availability, and performance guarantees. While budget hosting exists, developers must balance cost against reliability, network quality, and support availability. This analysis compares current 2026 pricing from six major providers, focusing on plans with exactly four virtual CPUs.

## Hetzner Cloud: The Cost Leader

Hetzner Cloud's **CPX21** plan represents the industry's lowest entry point for 4-vCPU infrastructure. At **$6.29/month** (billed annually), this plan includes:

- 4 vCPUs
- 8GB RAM
- 80GB NVMe SSD storage
- 20 Gbps network port
- 3.5TB monthly traffic included
- Locations in 7 geographic regions (Germany, Finland, US East, US West, Singapore, India, Japan)

Monthly billing costs $7.52, making the annual commitment necessary for the advertised rate. Hetzner's CPX21 occupies a unique position—its pricing undercuts competitors significantly, likely due to their vertical integration and infrastructure ownership model. The trade-off is no managed services; users deploy raw Linux or Windows instances and manage everything themselves.

Performance data shows Hetzner's AMD EPYC processors deliver consistent single-thread and multi-thread performance for development workloads. The 80GB NVMe storage suits development databases and small production applications.

## Vultr: Closest Mainstream Alternative

Vultr's **4GB Compute** instance costs **$12/month** (monthly billing available), featuring:

- 4 vCPUs
- 4GB RAM
- 80GB SSD storage
- 5 Gbps guaranteed network
- DDoS protection included
- 16 geographic locations

Vultr's pricing is roughly double Hetzner's, but the plan includes managed DDoS protection and slightly broader geographic distribution. The 4GB RAM matches Hetzner's offering, making this primarily an alternative when Hetzner's limited SLA or support model doesn't fit your requirements. Vultr supports monthly billing without penalty, useful for testing infrastructure before committing long-term.

## DigitalOcean: Premium Standard Option

DigitalOcean's **Standard 4GB** droplet costs **$24/month** (monthly billing), providing:

- 4 vCPUs
- 4GB RAM
- 80GB SSD storage
- 5 Gbps network throughput
- 4TB monthly transfer
- 1-click app marketplace

DigitalOcean's pricing reflects their focus on developer experience and managed features rather than raw specifications. The $24/month cost is double Vultr's despite identical vCPU and RAM allocations. Differentiators include their app marketplace (pre-configured software stacks), integrated firewalls, load balancers, and object storage integration. For teams using DigitalOcean's entire ecosystem, this integration value justifies the premium.

## Akamai Linode: Enterprise Focus

Akamai Linode's **Linode 8GB** plan costs **$48/month** (monthly billing), featuring:

- 4 vCPUs
- 8GB RAM
- 160GB SSD storage
- 40 Gbps network port
- 5TB monthly transfer
- Geographic availability in 12 regions

Linode's pricing is the most expensive among mainstream providers, quadruple Hetzner's cost. However, Linode offers enterprise-grade SLAs (99.9% uptime guarantee), managed backups, and stronger API reliability historically preferred by production applications. The increased RAM (8GB vs. 4GB) and storage (160GB vs. 80GB) add incremental value but don't fully justify the price gap for cost-conscious developers.

## OVHcloud: Limited 4-vCPU Availability

OVHcloud's pricing structure makes direct 4-vCPU comparisons difficult. Their **VPS M** plan costs **$6.99/month** (EU region, annually billed) with:

- 4 vCPUs
- 8GB RAM
- 40GB SSD storage
- €/month equivalent pricing varies by region

OVHcloud competes with Hetzner on price but offers slightly less storage. Geographic availability is Europe-centric, with limited US/Asia presence compared to other providers. Support quality reviews suggest adequate but slower response times than competitors.

## Cloudways: Managed WordPress Exception

Cloudways' **Managed Cloud Hosting** on **4vCPU Basic** infrastructure costs **$47/month** (annual billing), including:

- 4 vCPUs (underlying compute allocation)
- 4GB RAM minimum
- Managed WordPress/PHP stack pre-installed
- Automated daily backups
- CDN included (Bunny CDN)
- Email support and chat

Cloudways represents a different category—managed application hosting rather than raw VPS. The premium reflects operations management, WordPress optimization, security patching, and backup orchestration. For non-technical teams or WordPress-focused shops, this eliminates server administration overhead.

## Bottom Line

Hetzner Cloud's CPX21 at $6.29/month is unambiguously the cheapest 4-vCPU VPS available in 2026, but requires annual commitment and self-management. Vultr at $12/month offers the best balance of affordability and flexibility with monthly billing and DDoS protection. DigitalOcean ($24/month) suits developers prioritizing ecosystem integration. Akamai Linode ($48/month) targets production workloads requiring enterprise SLAs. Choose based on commitment flexibility, geographic requirements, and management overhead tolerance rather than vCPU count alone.
