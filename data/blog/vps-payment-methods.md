# VPS Providers That Accept Crypto, PayPal, and More

**TL;DR:** Most mainstream VPS providers accept credit cards and PayPal, but crypto options are limited. Vultr accepts Bitcoin and Ethereum; Hetzner Cloud and OVHcloud offer broader alternative payment methods. DigitalOcean and Akamai Linode remain card-primary, while Cloudways inherits Vultr's payment stack.

## Payment Method Overview

Choosing a VPS provider involves not just specs and performance, but how you'll pay for service. Developers and teams increasingly seek alternatives to traditional credit cards—whether for privacy, geographic restrictions, or technical preference. This article compares payment options across six major providers.

### Vultr

**Accepted Methods:** Credit/debit card, PayPal, Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDT (Tether), Google Pay, Apple Pay

Vultr stands out as the most crypto-friendly mainstream provider. Bitcoin and Ethereum payments are processed directly through their billing system, with no minimum purchase. They also accept Tether (USDT) on Ethereum and Tron networks.

Plan example: **Cloud Compute (Regular)** — $2.50/month (1 vCPU, 512 MB RAM, 10 GB SSD, 1 TB bandwidth)

PayPal integration works smoothly for monthly and annual billing. For crypto users, payments are non-reversible, and invoices are generated immediately upon transaction confirmation. This appeals to developers valuing transaction finality.

### DigitalOcean

**Accepted Methods:** Credit/debit card, PayPal

DigitalOcean does not offer cryptocurrency payments. However, their payment infrastructure is straightforward: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and PayPal all work without friction. International cards are accepted.

Plan example: **Droplets (Basic)** — $4/month (1 vCPU, 512 MB RAM, 10 GB SSD, 1 TB outbound bandwidth)

PayPal integration is full-featured; you can set it as a default payment method and enable auto-renewal. No cryptocurrency support limits options for privacy-conscious users or those in regions with restricted banking.

### Hetzner Cloud

**Accepted Methods:** Credit/debit card, PayPal, bank transfer (SEPA, wire), direct debit

Hetzner Cloud emphasizes bank-level payment flexibility, particularly for European customers. SEPA direct debit and wire transfers are available—useful for teams preferring traditional money movement or those with institutional procurement processes.

Plan example: **CX11** — €3.29/month (1 vCPU, 1 GB RAM, 25 GB NVMe SSD, 20 TB traffic)

No cryptocurrency support, but the bank transfer option provides an alternative to cards. This is valuable for organizations with restrictive payment policies. Monthly and annual billing cycles are both available.

### Akamai Linode

**Accepted Methods:** Credit/debit card, PayPal

Akamai Linode (formerly Linode, acquired by Akamai in 2022) accepts Visa, Mastercard, Discover, American Express, and PayPal. No cryptocurrency, no bank transfers.

Plan example: **Linode (Nanode 1GB)** — $5/month (1 vCPU, 1 GB RAM, 25 GB SSD, 1 TB outbound bandwidth)

PayPal integration includes auto-renewal and invoice management. For those requiring alternative payments, Linode's limitations are notable compared to Hetzner or Vultr. However, their billing portal is mature and reliable.

### OVHcloud

**Accepted Methods:** Credit/debit card, PayPal, bank transfer, some cryptocurrency (regional variation), gift cards

OVHcloud offers the broadest payment ecosystem among traditional providers. European customers can use SEPA direct debit; some regions support Bitcoin through payment processors. Gift cards allow prepayment without exposing payment methods.

Plan example: **VPS Starter** — €2.99/month (1 vCPU, 512 MB RAM, 10 GB SSD, 250 Mbps unmetered traffic)

Cryptocurrency availability varies by region and changes periodically; check their payment page for your location. Bank transfers are standard across EU operations. Annual prepayment often includes discounts.

### Cloudways

**Accepted Methods:** Credit/debit card, PayPal (inherited from Vultr infrastructure post-acquisition)

Cloudways, a managed VPS abstraction layer acquired by Vultr in 2021, inherits Vultr's payment infrastructure. Credit cards and PayPal are primary; cryptocurrency availability depends on ongoing integration.

Plan example: **DigitalOcean** (via Cloudways) **Starter** — $10/month (1 GB RAM, 1 vCPU, 25 GB SSD on DigitalOcean infrastructure)

Cloudways also supports Google Pay and Apple Pay. Their abstraction layer simplifies multi-cloud management, but payment methods align with their underlying provider partnerships.

## Bottom Line

**For cryptocurrency:** Vultr is the clear choice for Bitcoin and Ethereum; OVHcloud offers regional crypto support.

**For alternative traditional methods:** Hetzner Cloud and OVHcloud provide SEPA and wire transfer options, critical for teams with procurement restrictions.

**For simplicity:** DigitalOcean and Akamai Linode offer frictionless credit card and PayPal experiences without extra options—appropriate if you have a default payment method.

**For maximum flexibility:** OVHcloud edges ahead with the broadest payment ecosystem, though cryptocurrency availability requires verification per region.

Developers should verify current payment options on provider websites before account creation, as methods change and regional variations are common.
